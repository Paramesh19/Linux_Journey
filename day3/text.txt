Linux is a free, open-source operating system (OS) based on Unix,
 developed by Linus Torvalds in 1991. It manages hardware resources and runs
 software for devices ranging from servers and supercomputers to Android phone
s and personal computers. It is known for its security, stability, and flexibility,
 allowing users to modify and distribute the source code.
A Linux distribution is a complete operating system built around the Linux kernel,
 combined with system software, libraries, and applications.
 It provides users with everything needed to run Linux on different types of
 devices.
